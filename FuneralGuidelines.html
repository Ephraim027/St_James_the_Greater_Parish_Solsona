GUIDELINES FOR THE CELEBRATION OF
FUNERALS

GENERAL PROVISIONS

  1. A Memorandum of Agreement is to be signed between the faithful concerned and the parish office concerning the celebration or service being requested.
  2. Concerning TIME: Concerned persons, parties or families are to honor the time of appointment as stated in the Memorandum of Agreement entered into by them with the parish office. The parish office accepts various kinds of schedule in and outside the parish church-office compound as the parish office calendar of appointments permits. Attending to successive appointments is not unusual for the parish office.
  3. Concerning DECORUM: The faithful and visitors are to clothe themselves properly and modestly when participating at liturgical celebrations in the church. All who enter the church must honor the Divine Presence; hence, the need to dress accordingly.
  4. Concerning OFFERINGS OR DONATIONS: The celebration of baptism, wedding or church funeral is an occasion for the concerned family and their relatives and friends to DONATE towards the various needs of the parish. Individual donations made in the spirit of STEWARDSHIP, hence, from the generosity of their hearts, will be greatly appreciated.

POLICIES

  1. Only the funeral of a BAPTIZED CATHOLIC is normally celebrated in the parish church. Therefore, if the deceased person has been baptized in another parish, his or her BAPTISMAL CERTIFICATE is to be presented to the office before the funeral schedule is accepted.
  2. Schedule should be made as soon as possible or one (1) week before the funeral day, unless the wake is less than a week.
  3. As much as possible, at least two (2) immediate family members of the deceased should personally coordinate with the parish office. They should have the authority to enter into a Memorandum of Agreement with the parish office and be ready for a simple interview regarding the deceased.
  4. In accordance with the Catechism of the Catholic Church No. 1140, which teaches that the liturgy is celebrated by the whole Body of Christ, and mindful of the spirit of the Diocese of Laoag Pastoral Assembly I (1994) on non-discrimination, a funeral Mass appointment in the church is not exclusive to the family or party who first arranged the schedule. The celebration of a common funeral Mass for more than one deceased person is encouraged as a beautiful expression of our shared Catholic faith.
  5. The faithful departed is brought to the church only for the Funeral Mass. The church is not a place for viewing or floral offerings. These should be done during the wake at the home of the deceased or at the funeral home.
  6. At the conclusion of the Mass, which may extend to one (1) hour, the concerned family is given an additional twenty (20) minutes for a thanksgiving message and picture-taking.

Possible Days

Every day except Sunday and Monday. (The parish office is closed on Mondays, and parish personnel observe their day off. Tuesdays are generally reserved for clergy meetings, although accommodations may be made.)

Requirements

  1. Baptismal Certificate
  2. Burial Permit
  3. Copy of the Death Certificate

Nota Bene

These requirements should be submitted to the parish office when scheduling the funeral. If they are not yet available at that time, the concerned family should ensure that they are presented and submitted before the funeral is celebrated.

Post-Celebration Considerations

  1. To maintain the cleanliness of the church, the concerned family or their designated representatives should remove all decorations immediately after the picture-taking. Parish personnel are responsible only for sweeping the church floor.
  2. If the burial or interment takes place in the CATHOLIC CEMETERY, only ONE (1) WREATH, VASE OF FLOWERS, or FLOWER ARRANGEMENT may be left on the tomb. The proper disposal of all other decorations is the responsibility of the concerned family. For this reason, the parish office may require a refundable deposit of Php 1,500.00 (to cover possible clean-up expenses) or may temporarily keep the original Death Certificate until the required clean-up obligations have been fulfilled.
