GUIDELINES FOR THE CELEBRATION OF
WEDDINGS

GENERAL PROVISIONS

  1. A Memorandum of Agreement is to be signed between the faithful concerned and the parish office concerning the celebration or service being requested.
  2. Concerning TIME: Concerned persons, parties or families are to honor the time of appointment as stated in the Memorandum of Agreement entered into by them with the parish office. The parish office accepts various kinds of schedule in and outside the parish church-office compound as the parish office calendar of appointments permits. Attending to successive appointments is not unusual for the parish office.
  3. Concerning DECORUM: The faithful and visitors are to clothe themselves properly and modestly when participating at liturgical celebrations in the church. All who enter the church must honor the Divine Presence, hence, the need to dress accordingly.
  4. Concerning OFFERINGS OR DONATIONS: The celebration of baptism, wedding or church funeral is an occasion for the concerned family and their relatives and friends to DONATE towards the various needs of the parish. Individual donations made in the spirit of STEWARDSHIP, hence, from the generosity of their hearts, will be greatly appreciated.

CAVEAT! Schedule a celebration of marriage at least one (1) month before the desired date, AND before printing invitations and making arrangements with wedding coordinators.

DRESS CODE: No tube, "spaghetti"-strapped or similar dresses/gowns are allowed for the bride and her female secondary sponsors.

Ring and arrhae bearers and flower girls should be at least six (6) years old.

Possible Days

Every day except Sunday and Monday (The office is closed on Mondays, and office and church personnel take their day off.) N.B. Tuesdays are reserved for clergy meetings.

Requirements

Documentary (necessary for the acceptance of appointment):

  1. Baptismal Certificate (with annotation "For Marriage Purposes")
  2. Confirmation Certificate
  3. Marriage License
  4. Death Certificate of Spouse, if widow or widower
  5. Certificate of Freedom to Marry, if foreigner
  6. Requisite Dispensations and Permissions
  7. Marriage Banns

Others

  1. Creed, Basic Prayers, Sacraments, Commandments and Precepts of the Church memorized
  2. Pre-Cana Seminar (three (3) sessions)
  3. Reception of Requisite Sacraments (Baptism, Confirmation or Confession, as the case may be)
  4. Wedding Rehearsal

Decoration Limitations

No hammering of nails and no using of thumbtacks, staple wires, glue or similar items on the pews and other church furniture. WARNING! Fine: at least Php1,000.

Post-Celebration Considerations

To maintain the cleanliness of the church at all times, the couple or their designated representatives are to have all decorations from the church removed immediately after the picture-taking. Parish personnel are responsible only in sweeping the floor. WARNING! Fine: Php500.
