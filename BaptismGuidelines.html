GUIDELINES FOR THE CELEBRATION OF
BAPTISMS

GENERAL PROVISIONS

  1. A Memorandum of Agreement is to be signed between the faithful concerned and the parish office concerning the celebration or service being requested.
  2. Concerning TIME: Concerned persons, parties or families are to honor the time of appointment as stated in the Memorandum of Agreement entered into by them with the parish office. The parish office accepts various kinds of schedule in and outside the parish church-office compound as the parish office calendar of appointments permits. Attending to successive appointments is not unusual for the parish office.
  3. Concerning DECORUM: The faithful and visitors are to clothe themselves properly and modestly when participating at liturgical celebrations in the church. All who enter the church must honor the Divine Presence, hence, the need to dress accordingly.
  4. Concerning OFFERINGS OR DONATIONS: The celebration of baptism, wedding or church funeral is an occasion for the concerned family and their relatives and friends to DONATE towards the various needs of the parish. Individual donations made in the spirit of STEWARDSHIP, hence, from the generosity of their hearts, will be greatly appreciated.

Baptismal Days

Wednesday and Saturday, in the morning.

Schedule

  1. 8:30 – Registration
  2. 9:00 – Pre-Jordan Seminar (or earlier); Two (2) sessions
  3. 11:15 – Rite of Baptism

Requirements

Documentary: Birth Certificate; Permission from the proper parish priest, if the child's parents are not residents of the parish.

Personal (to PARENTS, if the candidate is below ten (10) years old; OR, to the ADULT CANDIDATE): Creed, Basic Prayers, Sacraments, Commandments, and Precepts of the Church memorized.

Dress Code

For the CANDIDATE – White.

For the REST – At least semi-formal attire: no shorts for men; no tube, "spaghetti"-strapped or similar dresses for women.

Qualifications to be a Sponsor (Can. 872)

  1. At least 16 YEARS OLD.
  2. A CATHOLIC who has been CONFIRMED, has RECEIVED THE BLESSED EUCHARIST, and leads a life of faith.
  3. Not bound by any canonical penalty legitimately imposed or declared.

Nota Bene

  1. There may be TWO (2) SPONSORS, one (1) male and one (1) female, who should present their Baptismal Certificate, Confirmation Certificate, and Certificate of First Communion (if available) to the parish office. Other persons are considered WITNESSES.
  2. A baptized non-Catholic and a Catholic who has not completed 16 YEARS may only serve as a WITNESS to the baptism.
  3. If possible, when scheduling a baptism, please bring a list of the sponsors and witnesses, indicating their respective religious affiliations.
